About Me

I am an Associate Professor and the Director of the GIS and Remote Sensing Research Center at Kastamonu University.

My academic journey began with a B.S. from Zonguldak Karaelmas University, followed by an M.S. in Forest and Natural Resources at Clemson University (SC, USA). I earned my Ph.D. in Forestry from Kastamonu University with a focus on advanced spatial technologies.

Currently, I serve as the Head of the Department of Nature Conservation and Biodiversity Management. I have also worked as a Visiting Scientist at Texas A&M University. My research primarily focuses on utilizing LiDAR, UAV photogrammetry, and satellite imagery to monitor forest ecosystems and land cover changes.
